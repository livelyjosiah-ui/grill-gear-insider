I Gave My Dad This for Father's Day. Now He Won't Stop Grilling.

I spoil my mom every year. My dad has gotten a card and some socks for five Father's Days in a row. This year I actually tried — and it kind of worked. Like, really worked.

avatar By Marlene Hart | Updated May 14, 2026 | 6 min read

Okay. I need to talk about something.

For Mother's Day this year, I gave my mom a spa day. The year before, brunch and flowers. The year before that, a personalized photo book that took me three weekends to make. I am, in my own humble opinion, a very good daughter on Mother's Day.

Father's Day? My dad has gotten a card from CVS and a pack of socks for, like... five years in a row.

He never says anything. He's the kind of dad who insists he doesn't want anything for his birthday, his Father's Day, or any holiday. And I've been taking that at face value because, honestly — I had no idea what to get him. The man has owned the same toolbox since 1996. He doesn't want a new watch. He doesn't read.

But this year I tried. And what I gave him has become the first Father's Day gift in our family's history that he actually uses every weekend.

How I even found it

A few weeks back I was scrolling TikTok and saw this video of an electric grill brush. Just a guy running it across his grill grates and the carbon and gunk literally peeling off in seconds. The video was so satisfying I watched it twice.

Now: I don't grill. I have never grilled. I have no relationship with a Weber. But my dad has been doing barbecue on Sundays since I was nine years old. Every Sunday. Ribs, brisket, sometimes chicken — always slow and serious. He doesn't talk much during it. He just sets up, lights up, and stands out there with a beer.

So I clicked.

Here's what I learned in fifteen minutes of research.

The wire brushes most people use are kind of medieval. They scratch up the grates, the bristles fall off and end up in the food (this is apparently a real and underrated safety thing), and they don't actually get the burnt stuff off. My dad's brush has visible bristles missing. I'd assumed that was normal. Apparently not.

Cleaning is also the worst part of grilling. Takes forever. Most dads just skip it, then end up cooking on a layer of last week's char.

The GrillMaster Pro is electric. You press a button, it spins a textured roller across the grate, and what used to take 30 minutes of scrubbing takes about 60 seconds. The heads pop off and go in the dishwasher. Replacement heads exist, so you literally never have to buy another brush.

I read that, looked at the price, and thought, oh. This is the gift.

So here are the five reasons I'm writing about it instead of doing literally anything else with my Friday night.

01

It fixes the actual worst part of his hobby.

Even if you don't grill, you understand this on a soul level. Anything food-related, the cleaning is the part nobody wants to do. Your dad has been scrubbing a grill the hard way for thirty years. This is the first thing I've seen that genuinely makes that part suck less. And it's the kind of upgrade he'd notice every single time he cooks.

02

He'd never buy this for himself. Like, ever.

Dads don't upgrade their tools. They use the same $8 grill brush from Walmart until it falls apart, then they buy another $8 grill brush. The entire point of a Father's Day gift is to give him the thing he wants but won't get for himself. This is exactly that.

(Also: he is going to show it to literally every guy who comes over. I have personally observed this for one month and counting.)

03

He's actually going to use it.

This is the one. I gave it to him on Father's Day morning. He opened it, looked at it for a second, and went straight outside to test it. (My mom mouthed "WOW" at me from the kitchen.) He came back in twenty minutes later and said, in his very dad way, "yeah, that's nice."

A week later, he started sending me pictures. Him grilling. Him with his buddies grilling. The cleaned grates. A close-up of a brisket like I'm supposed to know what to do with that information.

I rarely see my dad smile in photos. He smiled in three of them.
04

It's the gift that keeps giving. (Literally — for me.)

Because he's grilling more often now, I'm getting invited to more Sunday BBQs. He sends me ribs in tupperware when I visit. He texts me about new rubs he's trying. My mom told me he asked her, "is Marlene coming Sunday?"

Those are the moments you don't realize you're trying to buy until you're standing in one. I didn't pay $74 for a grill brush. I paid $74 to be on the invite list again.

05

If it flops, you ship it back. (It won't flop.)
